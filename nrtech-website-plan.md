## 13. Risk Mitigation & Contingency Planning

### 13.1 Content Production Risks

**Risk:** Internal team too busy to provide technical input/review

**Mitigation:**
- Schedule monthly 90-minute content planning sessions
- Create simple template for case study data collection
- Hire technical writer with chemistry background for first drafts
- Build 30-day content buffer ahead of publication schedule

**Risk:** Insufficient case studies due to client confidentiality

**Mitigation:**
- Anonymize all case studies (use "Regional Midwest Blender")
- Focus on technical problem-solving, not company specifics
- Create composite case studies combining multiple similar projects
- Develop hypothetical "technical challenges" content series

### 13.2 Technical Execution Risks

**Risk:** Website development delays

**Mitigation:**
- Select experienced Webflow/WordPress developer with portfolio
- Build in 2-week buffer in timeline
- Launch MVP first (core pages), enhance iteratively
- Use page builders to allow internal team minor updates

**Risk:** Platform technology limitations

**Mitigation:**
- Thoroughly vet platform capabilities before committing
- Choose platform with strong community support
- Ensure easy migration path if needed
- Avoid over-customization that creates technical debt

### 13.3 Market Response Risks

**Risk:** Low initial traffic/conversions

**Mitigation:**
- Set realistic 6-month runway before expecting significant results
- Plan for iterative optimization (A/B testing)
- Have email outreach campaign ready to drive initial traffic
- Consider minimal LinkedIn promotion to seed traffic
- Create compelling lead magnets to improve conversion rates

**Risk:** Competitors copying positioning/content

**Mitigation:**
- Focus on authentic case studies (hard to replicate)
- Emphasize actual capabilities, not just marketing
- Build personal relationships through consultation calls
- Develop proprietary tools/calculators as differentiators
- Patent or protect truly unique technical content

### 13.4 Compliance & Legal Risks

**Risk:** Technical claims challenged or misleading

**Mitigation:**
- All performance claims reference specific ASTM test methods
- Include disclaimers: "Results vary by application"
- Legal review of all technical whitepapers before publication
- Avoid absolute claims (use "typically," "can achieve")
- Document all test data backing published results

**Risk:** Copyright issues with images/content

**Mitigation:**
- Use only licensed stock photography or original images
- Create all technical diagrams in-house
- Properly attribute any third-party data/research
- Use royalty-free illustration libraries (unDraw, Humaaans)
